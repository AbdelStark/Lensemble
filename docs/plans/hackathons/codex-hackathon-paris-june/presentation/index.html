A JEPA world model predicts what the world will look like next — in latent space, not pixels. Surprise is the gap between what it predicted and what actually happened.

Down for the exact quantity · ~6 ms/step on CPU (>80 fps).

  • 192-d CLS latent, one vector per frame
  • 3-frame predictor window
  • no pixel decoder
0:15–0:35. The model predicted all of that — low surprise. Then the world did something it didn't expect → spike. "That spike is prediction error in latent space — literally the signal this model is trained on."

Surprise is one number per frame

surpriset  =  ‖ predicted next latent  −  actual next latent ‖²

A scalar prediction error in latent space — not a per-pixel heatmap. The CLS-latent model emits one 192-d vector per frame, so surprise is a recorded signal, not a picture.

Already implemented — web/federated-demo/lewm_probe.mjs. The recorder just runs it per step.

How: federated adapter continuation

i · each participant

Trains a 12,512-param adapter

on their local TwoRooms trajectories.

0.069% of the model
ii · clipped delta

Only the offset leaves

the adapter delta is clipped and sent — never raw data.

iii · aggregate

One shared revision

the mean of the clipped deltas updates the model.

The backbone stays frozen — only the adapter moves. Federated adapter continuation on a frozen checkpoint, not federated world-model training.

The honest mechanism. Spike #335: full-model federated training in-browser is a GPU-scale NO-GO, so the backbone is frozen and only the 12,512-param (0.07%) adapter trains and federates. If a judge asks "did you train a world model on phones?" — no, and say so plainly. That honesty is the differentiator at a research table.

The model is measurably less surprised.

Claim discipline

What we say

  • Federated adapter continuation on a frozen checkpoint
  • Scalar next-latent prediction error (CLS-latent)
  • +12.3% this run · +5.4% worst / +16.8% mean
  • Illustrative on the TwoRooms backbone

What we never claim

  • “Federated world-model training” / “the room trained the model”
  • A per-pixel or spatial surprise heatmap
  • Differential privacy or cryptographic proof on this path
  • Beats local-only · anomaly detector · paper-scale
On privacy: nothing leaves the device on this path, but we do NOT claim DP or secure aggregation here — that plumbing exists elsewhere in the repo, not in this path.
